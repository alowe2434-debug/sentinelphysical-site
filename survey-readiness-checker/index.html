Question 3 of 10
Do you have a complete asset inventory of every fire and smoke damper in your facility?
This includes dampers in mechanical rooms, ceiling plenums, above drop ceilings, and older wings of the building.
Question 4 of 10
When was your most recent fire and smoke damper inspection?
NFPA 80 requires inspection every 4 years. Environments with dust, grease, or corrosive materials require annual inspection of fusible link dampers.
Question 5 of 10
Is your inspector's CFSDI-1 certification (or equivalent) documented in your Life Safety Logbook?
PE.04.01.01 requires that contractor credentials are on file at your facility — not just held by the vendor. A surveyor may ask to see this certificate during a Physical Environment walk.
Question 6 of 10
Does your Maintenance Management Program reference PE.04.01.01?
Your MMP is one of the first documents a surveyor reviews. If it still cites EC.02.05.07 or LS.02.01.10, it is out of alignment with the current chapter structure.
Question 7 of 10
Is your team familiar with the SAFEST survey methodology?
SAFEST (Survey Activity Guide for the Environment of Safety and Technology) is the active TJC survey framework as of April 2026. It shifts from checklist-based to outcome-based assessment.
Question 8 of 10
Do you have any open damper deficiencies without a documented remediation timeline?
PE.04.01.01 (e) requires that deficiencies identified during inspection are tracked through remediation. Open items without a timeline are a Red-level finding.
Question 9 of 10
Does your facility operate Provider-Based Rural Health Clinics under the same CMS Certification Number?
Provider-Based RHCs that share a CCN with the parent CAH fall under the same Deemed Status umbrella. A Physical Environment deficiency at the parent facility creates exposure across all affiliated clinics.
